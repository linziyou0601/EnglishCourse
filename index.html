Old Faithful

The Old Faithful Fountain is not the largest fountain, nor the highest or the longest spraying fountain, but it is probably the most well-known fountain. Most fountains spray water at irregular intervals, making it impossible to predict the time of the next eruption.

Grand Teton National Park

Grand Teton National Park is adjacent to the southern end of Yellowstone National Park and covers an area of 485 square miles. The main river in the park: The Snake River runs through the north and south, winding several lakes.

Grand Prismatic Spring

Grand Prismatic Spring, also known as Boiling Lake. It is the largest hot spring in the United States and the third largest in the world. The color of the lake surface changes with the season. In spring, it changes from green to brilliant orange-red, and in summer, it appears orange, red, or yellow. In winter, it appears dark green.

Grand Canyon

Grand Canyon of the Yellowstone formed in rock and sediments about 160,000 to 140,000 years ago. Past and current hydrothermal activity altered and weakened the rhyolite, making the rocks softer. The Yellowstone River eroded these weakened rocks to deepen and widen the canyon, a process that continues today.
