Grand Prismatic Spring and Old Faithful

Grand Prismatic Spring, also known as Boiling Lake. It is the largest hot spring in the United States and the third largest in the world. The color of the lake surface changes with the season. In spring, it changes from green to brilliant orange-red, and in summer, it appears orange, red, or yellow. In winter, it appears dark green.

The Old Faithful Fountain is not the largest fountain, nor the highest or the longest spraying fountain, but it is probably the most well-known fountain. Most fountains spray water at irregular intervals, making it impossible to predict the time of the next eruption.

Rainbow Pool and The Black Sand Basin

The most distinctive aspect of the Lower Geyser Basin is the mud boiling spring, which means that the spring eyes are not clear water, but mud, and it is very thick mud. Among the many mud-boiled springs, the most representative is the colored pot fountain, a hot mud spring that continuously sprays high-temperature colored mud, like a swamp-like spring water, boiled by geothermal heat and boiling hot mud bubbles.

The Black Sand Basin in the Upper Geyser District has a series of gem-like brilliant geysers and colorful springs. The emerald pond is the most abundant and prestigious spring in it. Its colors are emerald, green, orange, yellow, etc. , A little Da Yanquan style. Milky pond is another famous spring, the center of the spring is blue, and it is surrounded by milky white, and there are a lot of dead trees around it. The Caiquan here have their own characteristics and are very beautiful.
